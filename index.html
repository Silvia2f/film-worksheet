College Short Film Worksheet

Short Film – Week 1: Story & Intent

Use this to pin down what you're making before you start shooting. It doesn't have to be perfect yet.

Core Idea

Describe your film idea in one paragraph.

Why This Story Matters

Why this idea? Why do you think it matters?

Intended Audience

What are your target audience characteristics? Avoid "everyone."

Tone

Select your story tone from below.

Tone

Point of View

Choose how the audience experiences the story.

We see through one character's eyes: The camera shows the story as if we are seeing it from that character's direct point of view.

We follow one character closely: The camera stays with one character most of the time, but we still see them from the outside.

We watch the story from the outside: The camera observes what is happening without locking into one person's experience.

A character speaks directly to the audience: Someone in the film talks to the viewer on purpose.

Mixed: The film combines more than one point of view.

Beginning

What happens in the beginning? Think about giving your audience context, introducing characters, and the problem or challenge.

Middle

What happens in the middle? Think about what develops, changes, or becomes more complicated.

End

What happens at the end? Think about how the story resolves, or what final feeling or image you want to leave with the audience.

Key Moments / Scenes

List specific scenes, moments, or shots you already know you want to include.

Visual Intentions

Describe the visual style. Think about lighting, color, camera movement, framing, and references.

Sound Intentions

Describe the sound. Think about music, ambient sound, silence, voiceover, or other audio choices.

Format Choice

Choose your format and think about how framing affects the meaning of the film.

Format Choice

What I'm Still Unsure About

What are you still unsure about? What feels unresolved or unclear right now?

What Feedback I Want

What kind of feedback would be most useful to you at this stage?

This is for planning, not locking yourself in. The project can still shift once you start making it.
